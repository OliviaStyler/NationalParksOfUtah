Capitol Reef

Capitol Reef National Park, situated in Utah, is a hidden gem renowned for its stunning red rock cliffs, canyons, and unique geological formations. The park's most iconic feature, the Waterpocket Fold, showcases a nearly 100-mile-long wrinkle in the Earth's crust, offering incredible scenic drives and hiking trails like the Capitol Gorge and Hickman Bridge.

Visitors can explore the historic Fruita district, where orchards planted by Mormon pioneers still thrive today, and enjoy picking and sampling fresh fruits during the harvest season. Adventurers can also embark on canyoneering expeditions, discovering hidden slot canyons and rock arches that dot the landscape. Capitol Reef National Park combines natural beauty, rich history, and exciting outdoor activities, making it a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts.
